About FinCalcHub

Our mission: professional-grade financial tools, free for everyone.

Our Mission

FinCalcHub was built with a simple belief: everyone deserves access to accurate, professional-quality financial tools — without paying for expensive software or being bombarded with misleading advice.

Financial decisions are among the most important choices you'll make in life. Whether you're buying a home, planning for retirement, paying off debt, or just trying to understand where your money goes, having the right tools makes all the difference.

We're here to level the playing field.

Why We Built FinCalcHub

We were frustrated with financial calculator websites that were slow, cluttered with ads, required sign-ups, or — worst of all — used incorrect formulas. Many popular calculators give wrong results because they use simplified or outdated math.

So we built FinCalcHub from scratch with three priorities:

  • Accuracy: Every formula is based on standard financial mathematics used by banks and lenders.
  • Speed: Pure vanilla JavaScript — no frameworks, no slow loading. Results are instant.
  • Simplicity: Clean, distraction-free design that works on any device.

Privacy Policy

We take your privacy seriously. Here's what you need to know:

  • No data collection: All calculations happen entirely in your browser. We never send your financial data to any server.
  • No registration: You never need to create an account or provide any personal information.
  • Cookies: We may use minimal cookies for analytics (page views only, no personal data) to understand how to improve the site.
  • Advertising: We display Google AdSense ads to keep the site free. Google may use cookies for ad personalization. You can opt out via Google's Ad Settings.
  • Third parties: We use Google Fonts (loaded from Google's servers) and Google Analytics for aggregate traffic statistics.

Disclaimer

The calculators and information on FinCalcHub are provided for educational and informational purposes only. They do not constitute financial, investment, legal, or tax advice. Results are estimates based on the information you provide and standard mathematical formulas.

Always consult a qualified financial advisor, loan officer, or other professional before making significant financial decisions. Actual loan terms, interest rates, and costs will vary based on your specific circumstances, lender policies, and market conditions.
