The 50/30/20 Budget Rule

The 50/30/20 rule is a popular budgeting framework popularized by Senator Elizabeth Warren in her book "All Your Worth." It divides your after-tax income into three categories:

  • 50% — Needs: Housing, groceries, utilities, transportation, insurance, minimum debt payments
  • 30% — Wants: Dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, shopping, vacations
  • 20% — Savings & Debt: Emergency fund, retirement savings, extra debt payments, investments

This framework provides flexibility while ensuring you're saving enough for the future. Adjust the percentages to fit your specific situation — some financial advisors recommend 20% savings for younger earners with smaller expenses.

Zero-based budgeting means assigning every dollar of income a specific purpose so that income minus expenses equals zero. You're not spending all your money — savings and investments count as expenses. This method forces intentional decisions about every dollar and is popular with people who want maximum control over their finances.
