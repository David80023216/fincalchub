How to Use the Mortgage Calculator

Our mortgage calculator makes it easy to estimate your monthly home loan payment. Follow these steps:

  1. Enter the home price — the total purchase price of the property.
  2. Enter your down payment — the amount you'll pay upfront (typically 3–20% of the home price).
  3. Enter the interest rate — the annual interest rate quoted by your lender.
  4. Select the loan term — typically 15 or 30 years.
  5. Click Calculate to see your monthly payment, total interest, and total cost.

Mortgage Payment Formula

The standard mortgage payment formula calculates your fixed monthly payment (M):

M = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] ÷ [(1+r)ⁿ − 1]

Where: P = Loan principal, r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12), n = Total number of payments (years × 12)

For example, on a $280,000 loan at 6.5% for 30 years: r = 0.065/12 = 0.005417, n = 360. Monthly payment ≈ $1,770.

Frequently Asked Questions

A good mortgage rate depends on current market conditions. Historically, rates below 4% are excellent. In 2024–2026, rates in the 6–7% range are typical for 30-year fixed mortgages. Your credit score, loan size, and down payment all affect your rate. Always compare multiple lenders to find the best offer.
A common guideline is the 28/36 rule: spend no more than 28% of your gross monthly income on housing costs and no more than 36% on total debt. For example, with a $6,000/month income, your target maximum housing payment would be $1,680/month. Many lenders allow up to a 43% debt-to-income ratio for approval.
Principal is the original loan amount you borrowed. Interest is the cost the lender charges for lending you money, expressed as an annual percentage rate. In the early years of your mortgage, most of your payment goes toward interest. Over time, more goes toward principal. This is called amortization.
A larger down payment directly reduces your loan principal, which lowers your monthly payment and the total interest you'll pay over the life of the loan. A 20% down payment also eliminates the need for Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI), which typically adds 0.5%–1% of the loan amount per year to your costs.
PMI is insurance that protects the lender — not you — if you default on your loan. It's typically required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home's purchase price. PMI usually costs between 0.5% and 1.5% of the original loan amount annually. Once you reach 20% equity in your home, you can request to have PMI removed.
